Onboarding
Answering this onboarding questionnaire will allow to determine which cybersecurity policies are relevant to your company.
Access
Company assets should be protected against any foreign or malicious access.
Active Directory
Network access and user authentication are better protected when the Active Directory infrastructure is secure.
Asset Management
Identification of corporate assets that store or handle sensitive information, as well as the identification of security concerns, depend on the management of technology assets.
Awareness
Employees that receive awareness training are more likely to have a culture of security and are encouraged to take safe actions when faced with online threats.
Business Continuity
The business can recover damaged systems or lost data with the help of a well-defined recovery plan.
Change and Configuration Management
The Change and Configuration Management policy aims to preserve system integrity and enable proactive change management by business and IT leadership.
Cloud Security
Cloud services need to be monitored to guarantee that business assets and data are protected.
Compliance and Auditing
The Compliance and Auditing policy aims to outline the steps involved in identifying, creating, managing proactively, and maintaining the company's cybersecurity compliance plan.
Data Protection
Requirements for the management, storage, and safe disposal of sensitive data are part of data protection, as are classification criteria.
Domain and DNS
Denial-of-Service attacks, network traffic rerouting, and DNS traffic tapping can all be prevented using domain and DNS protection.
Human Resources
Employees are important to cybersecurity because they can be simple targets for nefarious actions like social engineering attacks.
Incident Response
A well-organized incident response plan minimizes the harm that an attack does, lowers the likelihood of similar incidents in the future, and enables prompt attack identification.
Information Security Management
The first step in effectively managing cyber threats is to manage information security and risks within a cyber management framework.
Logging and Monitoring
Processes such as logging and system event monitoring must be carefully designed and set up in order to identify, produce evidence of, and respond promptly to security threats.
Microsoft 365
It is necessary to carefully configure the Microsoft Office 365 services and applications in accordance with manufacturer recommendations.
On-Premises Network
Communication channels that are housed and operated on corporate property should be set up carefully to prevent data breaches or leaks.
On-Premises Server
One of the primary targets for cyberattacks is thought to be on-premises servers. Businesses using on-premises servers ought to configure their systems according to best practices.
Operational and Maintenance
The purpose of the Operations and Maintenance policy is to ensure the business can maintain situational awareness of current and future performance.
Passwords and Secrets
The resources of a company are frequently accessed through passwords. Creating strong passwords and using them correctly are crucial to defending the business from brute-force attacks.
Physical Infrastructure
Maintaining the safety of an organization's physical infrastructure affects the overall security posture.
Privacy
The privacy policy's goal is to preserve gathered PII by guaranteeing its safe management, legal compliance, and protection of personal data.
Email and Messages
It is essential to safeguard company communications, accounts, and email systems against phishing, social engineering, and potential data leaks.
Endpoints and Mobile Devices
Preventing hackers from gaining access to user data or other company assets requires securing company workstations.
Environmental Control
Environmental controls define and prohibit unlawful access to firm facilities and physical assets. They also guard against theft, damage, and loss of physical assets.
Hosted Network
Means of communication managed by hosting services should be well configured to avoid data leaks or breaches.
Hosted Server
Hosted servers are considered one of the main targets for cyberattacks. Organizations using hosting server services should follow best practices configuration.
Threat Intelligence
Effective threat lifecycle management is made possible by this policy, which also supports detection, analysis, and reaction. It consists of organized methods for information gathering, processing, and sharing.
Vulnerability Management
Verifying that security rules are correctly and successfully implemented across all enterprise systems depends on vulnerability assessment.
Website
Due to their internet exposure, websites and web apps are vulnerable to being misused or tampered with by malicious parties if they are not well secured.
Service Provider and Vendor Management
Given that so many businesses in today's world rely on third parties for data management and IT infrastructure, security evaluation of service providers is essential.
Remote Access
As more workers work from home and remote access grows, businesses must contend with new cyberthreats.
Risk Management
To make sure that risks are identified, given priority, and appropriately managed, a risk management program is essential.
